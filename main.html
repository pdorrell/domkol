Domain Colouring using Canvas

Explanation

Domkol is an HTML5/Javascript/SVG/Canvas/JQuery application I am developing which displays a complex function as a colouring of the complex plain (a technique known as domain colouring), with additional interactivity to show values as a graph on a 1-dimensional subset of the domain (considered as a 2-dimensional Real number space). Currently it shows the function f(x) = x3.

Currently the only choice of 1-dimensional subset (i.e. curve) is a circle – I will probably add an option for viewing the values of the function on a chosen straight line.

The circle is controlled by two draggable handles: a centre handle that moves the circle as a whole, and an edge handle that controls the radius of the circle. The values are shown in terms of distance from the centre of the circle. Real is black; imaginary is blue. The scale of the mapping from the value of f to radius is controlled by the slider, which has a logarithmic scale.

Issues and Browser Compatibility

So far I have tested Domkol on the latest versions of various browser. The primary development browser is currently Chromium on Linux.

Known issues are:

  • It works on Chromium and Chrome on Linux and Windows, also (mostly) Internet Explorer on Windows.
  • I haven't worked out how to increase the slider length.
  • On Internet Explorer, the slider appears to the left instead of the right.
  • On Firefox (Windows or Linux), and Opera (Windows), the handles jump when dragging starts, making the dragging not very useable.
  • The handles and slider do not (yet) work on a touch screen. I tried Touch Punch, which worked for the slider, but not very well for the draggable handles.

TL;DR: it works best on Chrome/Chromium with a mouse.
