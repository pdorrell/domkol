Domkol: Identity Function (Linear Polynomial)

Function: Cubic Polynomial Quintic Polynomial Exponential Identity Function

Explanation

This example shows the identity function, i.e. f(z) = z. You can also alter the "zero" of the function, i.e. to show a function f(z) = z - a for arbitrary a.

The identity function is not enormously interesting, however, it serves as a useful reference point, to help understand the color scheme for the domain colouring, and also the representation of f(z) on the subset of the domain represented by the white circle.
